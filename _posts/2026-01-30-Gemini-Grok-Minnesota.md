* **Psychological Mechanism:** It is often driven by the **Just-World Hypothesis**, a cognitive bias where people want to believe the world is fair and predictable. * **In Practice:** This often looks like scrutinizing the victim’s behavior, dress, tone, or past history rather than focusing on the perpetrator’s choice to cause harm. (e.g., "Why didn't they just leave?" or "Why did they provoke him?")

**No. It is neurologically unlikely.**

* **The Amygdala Hijack:** When a human being perceives a legitimate and imminent danger, the brain shifts control from the **prefrontal cortex** (responsible for logic, complex language, and impulse control) to the **amygdala** and **limbic system** (responsible for survival instincts like fight, flight, or freeze).  
* **Cognitive Impact:** Under threat, the brain prioritizes speed and gross motor function over nuance and verbal articulation. A person in this state is flooded with cortisol and adrenaline. Their speech may become loud, fragmented, hyperbolic, or aggressive.
